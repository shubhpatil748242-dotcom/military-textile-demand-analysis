# Global Military Textile Demand Analysis and Forecast

## Project Overview
This project analyzes global military data to estimate and understand the demand for military textiles across different countries. Military textiles include materials used in uniforms, protective gear, camouflage fabrics, and tactical equipment.

The analysis combines global defense data to identify countries with the highest military textile demand and forecast potential demand growth by 2030.

---

## Data Sources

The project uses publicly available global datasets from:

- World Bank — Armed forces personnel and defense expenditure  
- Stockholm International Peace Research Institute (SIPRI) — Global military expenditure data

---

## Project Objectives

The main goals of this analysis are:

- Estimate military textile demand using armed forces personnel data
- Identify countries with the highest textile logistics requirements
- Analyze the relationship between defense spending and textile demand
- Visualize global patterns of military textile consumption
- Predict potential textile demand growth by 2030

---

## Methodology

The project follows these steps:

1. Data Collection  
2. Data Cleaning and Preprocessing  
3. Dataset Merging (World Bank + SIPRI datasets)  
4. Feature Engineering (Textile Demand Estimation)  
5. Exploratory Data Analysis  
6. Visualization  
7. Correlation Analysis  
8. Predictive Modeling for 2030 demand forecast

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Military Personnel Drives Textile Demand
Countries with larger armed forces require significantly higher volumes of military textiles such as uniforms, camouflage fabrics, and protective gear.

### 2. Defense Spending Influences Logistics Requirements
Countries with higher defense budgets tend to maintain larger military supply chains, increasing demand for textile materials used in military operations.

### 3. Military Textile Demand May Continue Growing
Forecast analysis suggests that military textile demand may increase by 2030 as countries expand defense capabilities and military personnel.
